Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most commonly asked questions about LinkLite and our URL shortening service.

What is LinkLite?

LinkLite is a modern URL shortening service that helps you transform long, unwieldy URLs into clean, compact, and easy-to-share links. Our service operates entirely client-side, ensuring your data remains private and secure.

How does LinkLite work?

LinkLite uses advanced algorithms to transform your long URLs into short links instantly. Simply paste your long URL into our shortener field, click the "Shorten" button, and your new short link is ready to be copied and shared anywhere.

Is LinkLite free to use?

Yes, LinkLite is completely free to use! We believe in providing essential tools without barriers, so you can shorten as many URLs as you need without any cost.

Where are my shortened links stored?

LinkLite stores your shortened links directly in your browser's local storage. This means your links remain private on your device and aren't stored on our servers. This enhances privacy but also means your links won't be accessible from other devices or browsers.

How long do my shortened links last?

Since your links are stored in your browser's local storage, they will remain available as long as you don't clear your browser data. LinkLite doesn't impose any expiration date on your shortened links.

Can I customize my shortened links?

Currently, LinkLite generates random short codes for your links to ensure uniqueness and security. We're considering adding custom link options in future updates, so stay tuned!

Is there a limit to how many links I can shorten?

There's no set limit to how many links you can shorten with LinkLite. However, browser local storage has capacity limitations that vary by browser. Most modern browsers provide sufficient storage for hundreds of shortened links.

Can I track clicks on my shortened links?

Currently, LinkLite focuses on providing a simple, privacy-focused shortening service without click tracking. We're exploring ways to add analytics features while maintaining our commitment to privacy in future updates.

What happens if I clear my browser data?

If you clear your browser data, including local storage, your shortened links will be deleted. We recommend exporting your important links before clearing browser data, a feature we're planning to add soon.

Which browsers are compatible with LinkLite?

LinkLite works with all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. The service relies on local storage, which is supported by all major browsers. For the best experience, we recommend keeping your browser updated to the latest version.

Still Have Questions?
